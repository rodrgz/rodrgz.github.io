This website exists because a resume is good at compression and bad at nuance.

It can say that I work on a credit limit system for a large bank, or that I spent years in
computational mechanics, but it says very little about how I think through problems or what kind
of engineering work sits behind those lines.

I wanted a place for the longer version: project notes, technical writing, research leftovers, and
the occasional post about backend systems, reliability, Linux, developer tooling, applied AI in
software delivery, agent and skill design, or the strange work of moving old
platforms into newer ones without breaking the business in the process.

My background is split between software engineering and mechanical engineering research, so the
topics here will probably reflect that. I tend to like work that can survive close inspection and
still make sense. That seems like a reasonable editorial policy.
